  04 UNIT

Case Studies, Focus Groups and Observations

Required Reading & Reflection

In Unit 4, I explored data collection methods like case studies, focus groups, and qualitative and quantitative observations. Each offers unique insights—case studies provide depth, focus groups reveal motivations, quantitative observation captures measurable data, and qualitative observation records natural behaviours without interference.
This unit's activities included preparation for a seminar: Case Study on Privacy focused on privacy considerations in data collection, where ethical issues often arise. I also submitted my Literature Review Outline and received valuable formative feedback that guided me in refining my research approach. The assigned readings provided a comprehensive understanding of the advantages and limitations of each data collection method, reinforcing the importance of aligning methods with specific research objectives.
A key takeaway was that combining methods enhances research reliability and depth. This approach has strengthened my ability to conduct structured, ethical, and well-suited research.

  05 UNIT

Interviews, Survey Methods, and Questionnaire Design

Required Reading & Reflection

In Unit 5, I learned how interviews, surveys, and questionnaires serve different roles in research. Interviews offer deep, personal insights, while surveys capture broader trends using structured questionnaires. I also discovered the value of pre- and post-testing for evaluating interventions, and how question design—open-ended for qualitative and closed-ended for quantitative—shapes research outcomes.
As part of the unit activities, I completed Reflective Activity 2, where I evaluated suitable data collection tools for my ongoing research project, considering how interviews could capture detailed experiences while surveys could validate trends. The Wiki activity (post, original questionnaire, improved questionnaire) on questionnaire design was particularly enlightening, as it highlighted the dangers of poorly structured questionnaires and provided strategies to craft effective ones that avoid ambiguity and capture accurate data.
Overall, this unit reinforced the importance of selecting appropriate data collection methods based on research objectives.

  06 UNIT

Quantitative Methods - Descriptive and Inferential Statistics

Required Reading & Reflection

In Unit 6, I explored quantitative methods, focusing on descriptive and inferential statistics. These methods are crucial in data-driven decision-making across scientific research and business applications. Descriptive statistics summarise data, helping to identify patterns and trends, while inferential statistics allow researchers to conclude a population based on sample data. The concepts of location (mean, median, mode) and dispersion (range, variance, standard deviation) were particularly insightful, as they provide foundational tools for understanding data distributions.
As part of the e-Portfolio activities, I engaged with Unit 6 reading materials. I also worked on the Seminar Preparation Questions for Unit 7, which involved applying descriptive statistics and hypothesis testing.

  07 UNIT

Inferential Statistics and Hypothesis Testing

Required Reading & Reflection

In Unit 7, I delved into inferential statistics and hypothesis testing, essential tools in data analysis. Inferential statistics allow researchers to conclude a population based on a sample, helping to identify trends and relationships. Unlike descriptive statistics, which summarise data, inferential methods use probability to make predictions and test hypotheses. Understanding probability distributions is crucial, as they determine the validity of statistical conclusions. Hypothesis testing, a fundamental aspect of this unit, involves evaluating assumptions about data through statistical significance.
During this unit, I engaged in various e-Portfolio activities, including the Hypothesis Testing Worksheet (Exa 7.4F; Exa 7.6B) and Summary Measures Worksheet (Exa 7.1B; Exa 7.2B; Exa 7.3D), which required me to apply inferential techniques to real datasets. Additionally, I participated in Collaborative Learning Discussion 2. Preparing for seminar 7 Inferential Statistics Workshop further reinforced my ability to conduct hypothesis tests and evaluate statistical evidence effectively. These activities have strengthened my analytical skills, equipping me with a deeper understanding of how inferential statistics can transform raw data into meaningful conclusions. A significant milestone in this unit was the submission of my assessment on Literature Review, which required synthesising relevant academic sources to support my research.

  09 UNIT

Validity and Generalisability in Research

Required Reading & Reflection

Unit 9 explored the essential concepts of validity, generalisability, and reliability, which are crucial for robust research design. Validity assesses whether the research measures what it intends to, while reliability focuses on the consistency of the measurement process. A key insight was that reliability is a prerequisite for validity—a measurement cannot be valid if it is not reliable.
I completed the Charts Worksheet, which involved analysing and visualising data to present findings effectively. This activity reinforced the importance of clear data presentation. Additionally, I completed my Summary Post on Collaborative Learning Discussion 2, incorporating feedback from my peers to reflect on my initial discussion.
These activities deepened my understanding of research credibility, helping ensure my proposal on machine learning in diabetes diagnosis is valid, reliable, and impactful.
